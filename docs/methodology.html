How We Score Risk

DRA v3.0 is a discrete, criterion-based methodology. Strategies are graded on a 0/1/2 ladder — not a continuous score — with a single failing cell ratcheting the whole rating to the worst stage. Every assumption is public, every attestation is auditable.

Step 1

Decompose the Strategy

Every strategy is broken down into up to three layers, each representing a different level of the stack you are exposed to.

◈

Asset

The underlying token itself. Is USDC safe? How stable is stETH? What backs wBTC?

◎

Market

The lending or borrowing venue where the asset is deployed — Aave, Compound, Fluid, etc.

◆

Vault

The strategy vault or CDP that allocates capital across markets — Morpho, Yearn, Maker, etc.

Not every strategy uses all three. The applicable layers are selected by composition mode:

Mode Applicable layers Typical example
Aasset · market · vaultVault routing through a money market (Morpho on Aave)
Basset · vaultNative vault that holds the asset directly
Casset · marketDirect deposit into a money market (Aave reserve)
Dasset · meta-vault · underlying vaultsVault of vaults — the worst underlying vault still ratchets the rating

Step 2

Score on Three Components

Each layer is independently evaluated on three components. Together they form a 3 × 3 matrix of stages.

🔒

Security

Smart-contract maturity, audit coverage, bug bounty, and clean-track-record (Lindy).

⚙

Operations

Governance posture, timelocks, oracle quality, documentation, and disclosure.

⚖

Strategy & Economics

Peg, collateral, liquidity depth, parameter conservatism, and strategy complexity.

Every cell of the matrix carries a list of named, monotonic criteria — concrete, falsifiable yes/no questions. Stage 1 has its own criteria; Stage 2 adds further criteria on top. The full registry lives in methodology/criteria.py.

Security Operations Strategy & Economics

MarketStage 0 / 1 / 2Stage 0 / 1 / 2Stage 0 / 1 / 2
VaultStage 0 / 1 / 2Stage 0 / 1 / 2Stage 0 / 1 / 2

Step 3

Three Stages, Ratchet to the Worst

There is no continuous 0–10 number. A cell sits at one of three discrete stages.

0

Unproven

At least one Stage 1 criterion is unsatisfied or unattested. The cell does not meet a baseline of safety.

1

Baseline

Every Stage 1 criterion is satisfied. The cell meets a baseline of safety and disclosure.

2

Hardened

Every Stage 1 and Stage 2 criterion is satisfied. The cell is hardened — multi-audit, long-Lindy, conservative.

Cells, layers, and the strategy roll up by the same simple rule — min-of-children:

component_stage = max N where every criterion at stages 1..N is satisfied
layer_stage = min(component_stage) over the three components
strategy_stage = min(layer_stage) over the layers applicable to the mode

This is the ratchet: a single failing cell pulls the whole strategy down to the worst stage. There is no averaging, no rounding, no compensating a 2 with a 0. The roll-up logic is implemented in methodology/compose.py.

Example. A strategy with the matrix below:

SecurityOperationsStrategy & Economicslayer
Asset2222
Market2211
Vault1010

The single zero in vault.operations drives vault to Stage 0, which drives the strategy to Stage 0 regardless of how strong the asset and market layers are.

Trust Model

Default to Worse

No single rater defines the truth. Multiple independent providers submit attestations against the named criteria. Each attestation is one of verified, violated, or unknown, and carries the source plus a short evidence string.

Attestations are merged with one rule, taken straight from methodology/merge.py:

satisfied = bool(verifications) and not violations

In words:

  • ·One violated beats any number of verified — opacity-resistant, but also pessimistic when raters disagree.
  • ·No attestations at all → the criterion is unsatisfied. Missing data is a risk factor, not a neutral fact.
  • ·Disagreements are not averaged away. They surface in the per-entry attestation list and are the entry-point for the public challenge process.
Why this rule? Averaging incentivises raters to file shallow verified attestations to dilute red flags. Default-to-worse forces the rating to surface at least one source of evidence that it is wrong, in the open.

Independent Raters

Who Files Attestations

A rater can cover as little as one criterion in one cell — there is no requirement to be exhaustive. Each adapter declares its supported criteria, fetches its data, and emits attestations. The current production raters in this repo:

Rater Where it attests Source
Pharos Asset layer for stablecoins — bluechip ratings, peg stability, deep liquidity from totalTvlUsd. providers/pharos.py
Yearn Risk Curation Market and Vault layers — security / operations / economics derived from Yearn's published curation reports (1–5 scale bucketed into S1/S2 thresholds). providers/yearn.py
Philidor Vault layer (and Market when philidor_fill_market_from_vault is set) — audits, timelocks, curator accountability. providers/philidor.py
Vaultscan All three layers — numeric scores from the Vaultscan Supabase index, thresholded against the published S1/S2 cut-offs. providers/vaultscan.py

Adapter weights are equal in v3.0 — the resolution rule is binary, so weights do not currently feed into the merge. They are reserved for future tie-break or confidence work.
